Market and Consumer Definitions.

As TouchTalk is an alternative communication device, it resides within the Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) device market. AAC devices enable users to supplement or replace speech via augmentative and alternative communication devices or medium, with TouchTalk facilitating this via the projection of communication images that the child can point to, while simultaneously providing stimulative fun.

TouchTalks consumerbase would fall in line with those catered by other manufacturers of AAC


products, specifically the UK’s SEND educational sector and their respective stakeholders. This includes, but is not exclusive to:


  • State funded educational institutes (those funded by the UK government or local councils)
  • Independent educational institutes.
  • NHS Services, namely the Speech and Language departments nationwide.
  • Independent/contracted SEND educators

Our user demographic that the above consumerbase serve consist of children that fall under SEND and meet the following criteria:

  • Aged between 4-13.
  • Are on a Communication and Interaction SEND educational framework.
  • Are recognised to enjoy sensory stimulation of both a visual and physical nature ie not easily overstimulated.
  • Are regular users of Communication Boards.



Market Research.

Total Addressable Market (TAM).


Our TAM consists of the global AAC Device market. This market has been estimated for 2024 to be valued at: 0.35 Billion USD. This is due to the worldwide recognition of those needing and using AAC devices. The American market share is the largest within this evaluation, however, the UK proportion of autistic children and adults to the population is one of the largest in the world and continuing to grow, showing the potential for our market share to increase. Within the global market, the forecasted market value by 2029 is estimated at 0.6 Billion USD by 2029.


Service Addressable Market (SAM).


To allow TouchTalk to eventually address the global market, we are first targeting the UK market for AAC (see market strategy). To evaluate the UK market, the distribution of SEND/ASN/ALN children (various terms used across UK respective countries) was researched:


  • Over 1.5 million pupils in England are identified as having SEND. Autism continues to be the primary need for the majority of children with an EHCP, and Speech, Language and Communication Needs remains the most common type of need for SEN support (2023).
  • Within Scotland, the number of pupils with ASN, such as autism, dyslexia and mental health problems in 2023 has reached a record high of 259,036.
  • In Wales, they recorded 63,089 pupils with additional learning needs (ALN) or special educational needs (SEN) in 2023.
  • As of 2022-2023, more than 66,000 pupils in Northern Ireland had some form of SEN

As we will be catering to the educators of these children, namely schools, councils and independent institutions, we must estimate the number of units sold based off the number of potential users rather than consumers. From the above statistics, we can estimate the total number of UK SEND children at 1.9 million.


Given these statistics and our preliminary revenue, we can estimate our SAM as: £246M.


Beachhead Market.


The above statistics consider only the total number of SEND children in the UK. However, for TouchTalks' beachhead market, we need to target users of AAC devices rather than individuals on various Education, Health, and Care (EHC) plans.

Specific statistics on AAC usage for children within the UK aren't definitive due to it being an under-documented subsection of the autistic spectrum. However, we can consider the following:


  • The most common type of need for those with an EHC plan is autistic.
  • Majority of those with SEN support (59%) have a primary type of need of Speech Language and Communication needs (SLCN).


Considering that AAC is more commonly used with SLCN support and exercises, we can further approximate our market to 1.14 million children across the UK. However, with improved identification of children with ASD and projected global estimation of AAC device users considered in our TAM, we can see this number growing drastically over the next 5 years.


With this, we define our beachhead market at £158.5M

Our Next Generation Mid-Tech AAC Solution.

  • TouchTalk is addressing the mid tech AAC range of devices- this is a surprisingly untapped market due to most devices targeting the high momentary high tech devices or taking advantage of the simplicity of the low tech device market.
  • Within the mid tech market, the main options are currently dated, unexciting products, some of which are greatly overpriced.
  • Within the UK alone there is no mid tech AAC device focused competitor.
  • TouchTalk is a unique product in the current overall AAC market - no product aims to provide both stimulatory sensory fun as well as act as a communicative device
  • There are currently no AAC devices that use communicative cards that are not tablet/flat device centred; TouchTalk would be unique as a projection based AAC device








Marketing Strategy.

Due to the limited innovation within the AAC market and market gap discovered within the mid tech AAC device market, we knew the importance of grasping this opportunity for TouchTalk by entering the market with a strong market strategy.




Key elements of our market strategy:

  • Utilising our USP
  • Invest in marketing and promotion heavily for initial year
  • Targeting local educational institutions first
  • Ensure we implement feedback from educators
  • Seek product evolution to meet feedback demands and establish ourselves as a key listener to consumers

USP
Marketing and Promotion
Local Institutions

TouchTalk’s Unique Selling Point (USP) is a culmination of the following:
  • It is a unique AAC product on the current global market: no other AAC device utilises projection in any way.
  • There is limited new technology within the mid tech AAC submarket - most are obsolete technologies as discovered during market research hence can utilise.
  • This would be the first product that attempts to provide communicative means while also allowing for sensory stimulation during use, aiming to tackle keeping children attentive to educators working with them but also provide sensory stimulatory fun.
In order to grasp institutions' attention, we understand the fundamental need for advertising and promotion of TouchTalk. Considering our initial customer base will consist purely of institutions, the initial marketing will consist of:

    Video production
  • Professional video shoot and editing for 2-3 marketing videos, aiming to be used on our website and shortened clips for digital ads

    • Demonstrations to Institutions
  • Tesseract understands the importance of human connection and the appreciation of meeting in person within this digital age, hence for a product aiming to aid such a community of people we would be travelling to potential customers i.e. educational institutions and councils to discuss TouchTalks benefits for SEND children and provide product demonstrations.

    • Website & Digital Ads
  • We would ensure that TouchTalk was suitably advertised online by websites of similar nature to our end users and institutional customers. We would also invest in a strong website detailing TouchTalks functionality and suitability for various end users, with clear instructions for usage and a helpline for educators or parents in need of product assistance.
Due to initial startup costs and establishment of the product, we are taking the approach of focusing marketing and sales to Tesseracts’ local city of Edinburgh and it’s surrounding towns.
This will allow us to:
  • Save on travelling costs within the first year.
  • Save on global distribution and manufacturing costs within the first year.
  • Utilise Scotland's leading Disability services and institutional funding for SEND children.
  • Establish TouchTalk as a market leader in mid tech AAC within Scotland and expand to the rest of the UK.

Listen and Implement Feedback.

At Tesseract, we understand that this product's essential value will be in its ability to provide a communicative medium for non verbal/partially verbal children, hence it is important for us to respect and listen to educators and specialists feedback regarding our product if we wish it to fulfil its true value potential.


While crafted with intent to educate and provide sensory fun, as we are not the main users of TouchTalk we must understand and respect feedback from our end users and educators with regards to its usage, and seek to improve the product where we can. We have made this one of our company's pledges : to respect, listen and change products in order to provide their greatest value to our end users and allow educators to use them with greater ease.


By communicating this pledge with institutions, educators and parents, we hope to establish ourselves as a company that cares about the actual needs of its end users, that can adapt to change and is continually working to produce products that truly work, educate and can be used with joy.
