The Role of Large Language Model (LLM) Technology

25 March 2024

Krithikha Sivaraj

Market Research

In today's rapidly evolving market, the integration of Language Model (LLM) technology has redefined the boundaries of human-computer interaction.

For businesses, the appeal of Artificial Intelligence (AI) lies in its potential to craft an intelligent, and autonomous brand representation. LLMs offer the promise of transforming brands into dynamic entities, necessitating a focus on soft skills such as branding, understanding consumer behavior, and psychology to effectively leverage this technology. This transformative potential necessitates a strategic approach to LLM implementation, encapsulated in four key elements: Define, Align, Flow, and Run. From defining user experiences to aligning strategies with market innovation and ensuring seamless data flow and optimal technical operations, these elements form the cornerstone of successful LLM integration:

  • Define: This stage revolves around defining the user experience in alignment with the brand and its objectives. It entails meticulous planning of how LLMs interact and behave within the proposed system.

  • Align: Strategic alignment with market innovation is crucial for staying ahead of the curve. By tracking and predicting market innovations, businesses can strategically upgrade their roadmaps and ensure continued relevance.

  • Flow: Seamless data flow is imperative for the efficacy of LLM-based systems. Ensuring that language models are grounded in accurate data is fundamental to their functionality and reliability.

  • Run: Technical operations and infrastructure play a vital role in the successful execution of LLM models. Optimal performance and cost efficiency are achieved by running models on robust infrastructure tailored to meet specific needs.



While LLM innovation presents remarkable opportunities for various industries, including communication and accessibility, it's essential to recognize that not all products necessarily integrate this technology. In the case of TouchTalk by Tesseract, here's why our product does not directly incorporate LLM technology:

TouchTalk prioritises visual communication through holographic projection of communication tiles. Unlike LLMs, which primarily focus on natural language processing and generation, our product empowers non-verbal individuals by providing a tangible and intuitive means of expression. Rather than interpreting and generating language, TouchTalk facilitates communication through pre-defined visual cues, tailored specifically to meet the needs of users with special requirements.

The TouchTalk app allows users to select from a range of specialised communication images designed for individuals with Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND). These images serve as communication aids, offering a structured and accessible way for non-verbal individuals to convey their thoughts and needs. While LLMs excel in understanding and generating human-like text or speech, TouchTalk focuses on providing a curated library of visual communication tools, enhancing accessibility and inclusivity for its users.

Central to TouchTalk's functionality is its utilisation of holographic projection technology. Through projection onto surfaces, users can interact with communication tiles in a dynamic and engaging manner. This innovative approach emphasizes the visual aspect of communication, offering a multi-sensory experience that goes beyond traditional methods. While LLMs leverage advanced algorithms for language processing, TouchTalk leverages holographic technology to facilitate communication through visual mediums.

While LLM technology holds immense potential in revolutionising communication and accessibility, TouchTalk offers a distinct approach tailored to the needs of non-verbal individuals through its emphasis on visual communication, specialised images, holographic projection, and tailored solutions. TouchTalk provides an innovative platform for empowering individuals to express themselves effectively in a way that traditional language models may not address.
